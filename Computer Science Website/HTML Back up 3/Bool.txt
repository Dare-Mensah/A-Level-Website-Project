	<h2>Simple Logic Gates and Truth Tables</h2>
	<p>There are a number of different logic gates that are each designed to perform a different operation
	in terms of output. We will look at NOT, AND, OR and XOR gates.</p>
<hr>
	<h2>NOT Gate</h2>
	<p>The NOT gate is represented by the symboil below and inverts the input. The small circle denotes an inverted 
	input.</p>
<hr>
	<h2>AND Gate Conjuction</h2>
	<p>The truth table reflects the fundamental property of the AND gate: the output of A AND B is  1 
	only if input A and input B are both 1.
<hr>
	<h2>OR Gate (Disjunction)</h2>
<hr>
	<h2>XOR Gate (Exclusive disjunction)</h2>
	<p>The XOR stands for exclusive OR, meaning that the output will be true if one or other input 
	is true, but not both.
<hr>
	<h2>Rules Of Boolean Algebra</h2>
<ol>
	<p>General Rules</p>

	<li>X AND 0 = 0</li>
	<li>X AND 1 =X</li>
	<li>X AND X = X</li>
	<li>X AND NOTX = 0</li>
	<li>X OR 0 = X</li>
	<li>X OR 1 = 1</li>
	<li>X OR X = X</li>
	<li>X OR NOTX = 1</li>

	<p>Commulative Rules</p>

	<li>X AND Y = Y AND X</li>
	<li>X OR Y = Y OR X</li>

	<p>Associative Rules</p>

	<li>X AND (Y AND Z) = (X AND Y) AND Z</li>
	<li>X OR (Y OR Z) = (X OR Y) OR Z</li>

	<p>Distributive Rules</p>
	
	<li>X AND (Y OR Z) = (X AND Y) OR (X OR Z)</li>
	<li>(X OR Y) OR (W OR Z) = (X AND W) OR (X AND Z) OR (Y AND W) OR (Y AND Z)</li>
	
	<p>Absorption Rules</p>
	
	<li>X OR (W AND Y) = X</li>
	<li>X AND (X OR Y) = X</li>
	
	<p>Double Negation</p>
	
	<li>X = NOT NOTX</li>
</ol>
